Resume Engine — User Guide

What this is

Paste a job description, get a tailored resume and cover letter in about 30 seconds. The AI rewrites your summary, reorders skills to match the JD, re-emphasizes your bullet points, and writes a matching cover letter — then exports all four files (PDF + Word for each).

Honest by design. The AI is instructed to never invent skills, experiences, or metrics you don't already have. It only reorders, rewords, and re-emphasizes what's already on your resume.

What you need

  • A modern web browser (Chrome, Edge, Firefox, Safari, Arc)
  • An Anthropic API key — free to create, costs ~$0.03 per job. Get one at console.anthropic.com/settings/keys
  • Your existing resume (PDF/Word/TXT) — or just your info if starting fresh

First-time setup

When you open the engine for the first time, a welcome screen appears with three options:

  1. Upload your resume — drop in a PDF, Word, or TXT file and the AI parses it for you. Fastest option. (Works with text-based PDFs only — scanned ones won't work.)
  2. Fill a guided form — 4-step wizard if you don't have a resume handy or want to start fresh.
  3. Start blank — skip to the JSON editor. For power users.

Adding your API key

When you upload a resume, the engine will ask for your key. Once entered, it's saved locally — you won't need to paste it again. You can also add or update it anytime in the Settings tab.

The 30-second loop

Once your base resume is loaded, every new application takes about 2 clicks:

  1. Copy the full job description from LinkedIn, Indeed, Glassdoor, or wherever. Include both responsibilities and requirements — more text means better tailoring.
  2. Open the engine, stay on the Tailor tab (it's the default).
  3. Paste the JD into the big text box. Optionally fill in company name and hiring manager — or leave blank and the AI will detect the company.
  4. Click Generate Resume + Cover Letter (or press ⌘ + Enter on Mac / Ctrl + Enter on Windows). Wait 15–40 seconds — both documents generate in parallel.
  5. Review the preview on the right. Toggle between Resume and Cover Letter at the top. Click "What changed?" to see the AI's edits.
  6. Click Download All 4 Files. You get resume PDF, resume Word, cover letter PDF, cover letter Word — all in your downloads folder.

Customizing the cover letter

The default tone (professional) and length (medium, ~280 words) suit most applications. To regenerate with different settings, open the Cover Letter tab:

  • Tone: Professional · Enthusiastic · Direct · Formal
  • Length: Short (~200w) · Medium (~280w) · Long (~380w)
  • Notes: Mention a referral, your availability, a specific product you love — anything contextual

Editing your base resume

Open the Edit Resume tab. Your data is shown as JSON. Fix typos, add a new job after a promotion, update contact info — then click Save Changes. Future tailorings use the corrected version.

Header buttons

  • Reset — restores your base resume, undoing the current tailoring
  • New Resume — re-opens the welcome flow to import a different resume entirely
  • Help — opens this guide anytime

Paste the entire job description

More text = better tailoring. Don't just paste the title and one line — include responsibilities, required skills, nice-to-haves, and company context.

Keep your base resume thorough

The AI can only re-emphasize what's already there. If you used Vue.js once but didn't mention it, it can't help on a Vue job. Add everything you've genuinely worked with — the engine picks what's relevant per JD.

Always review before sending

Read the output carefully. Check for:

  • Names and metrics that should be preserved exactly
  • Awkward phrasing or overly formal sentences in the cover letter
  • Skills the JD asked for that you don't have — confirm the AI didn't slip any in

Add personal touches to the cover letter

Hiring managers can smell pure AI output. Edit at least one sentence to mention something specific the AI couldn't know: a recent product launch, a mutual connection, a blog post the company published. This one touch dramatically improves response rates.

Be honest about skill gaps

If a job requires a skill you don't have, don't fake it. Use the Notes field in the Cover Letter tab to address it openly: "Comfortable ramping on X — actively learning given my deep Y foundation." More credible than silence, and won't blow up at the technical screen.

The Sonnet model is the sweet spot for nearly everyone. Opus is overkill for routine applications; Haiku occasionally produces less polished cover letters. The default is set right.

How pricing works

The engine itself is free. You pay Anthropic for the AI calls. Costs depend on which model you choose (set in the Settings tab):

Claude Haiku — fastest, cheapest ~$0.01 per job
Claude Sonnet — recommended balance (default) ~$0.03–0.05 per job
Claude Opus — highest quality ~$0.15–0.25 per job

Each "job" is one full Quick Generate (resume + cover letter). Most people find Sonnet's quality is more than enough for routine applications.

A $5 credit on Anthropic lasts most people 100+ applications. Add credit through the Anthropic console — it's a one-time top-up, not a subscription. You're billed only as you use it.

Saving money

  • Use Haiku for first drafts; upgrade to Sonnet only if quality drops
  • Use the "Advanced — tailor resume only" option (in the Tailor tab) when you only need a fresh resume, not a new cover letter
  • Edit your base resume in the Edit tab rather than re-uploading — uploads cost extra to parse

Where your data lives

  • The engine runs entirely in your browser. There is no server collecting your resume, JDs, or applications.
  • Your API key is stored in your browser's localStorage. It is never sent anywhere except directly to Anthropic when you click Generate.
  • Anthropic sees your resume + JD when you call the API — they're the AI provider. Their privacy policy is at anthropic.com/legal/privacy. By default, consumer API data isn't used to train models.
  • Clearing your browser data wipes everything. Resume, API key, settings — all gone. Always keep an exported Word version as backup.
Don't share this HTML file with your API key still saved. To share the tool, use a fresh shareable copy — or clear your browser data on this device first.

Will recruiters know?

Probably not for the resume — the engine reorders and rephrases your existing content rather than generating fresh text. The cover letter is more detectable; that's why editing it before sending matters. If your resume reflects your actual experience, no one can call it fake.

The PDF upload says "Could not extract text"
Your PDF is probably a scanned image. Open it in Word or Google Docs, export as DOCX, and upload that instead. Or use the guided form.
I get "API 401" or "API 403" errors
Your API key is invalid or expired. Go to Settings, paste a fresh key from console.anthropic.com/settings/keys, save again.
I get "API 429" or rate limit errors
You're hitting Anthropic's rate limit (common on free tier or low spend tier). Wait a minute and retry, or add more credit to your account.
The tailored resume changed my dates or company names
Shouldn't happen — the prompt forbids it. If it does, click Reset, try again. If it persists, check the changes log and fix manually in the Edit Resume tab.
The cover letter sounds too generic or too AI-ish
Try the Direct or Enthusiastic tone — they tend to produce more distinctive output than the default Professional. Add a specific note in the Notes field. Most importantly: edit at least one sentence yourself before sending.
Can I use this on my phone?
Yes, but the preview is much better on a desktop or tablet. On mobile the layout collapses to a single column — fine for quick edits, awkward for full review.
I uploaded my resume but the parser got something wrong
Open the Edit Resume tab and fix the field directly in the JSON. Click Save Changes. Future tailorings use the corrected version.
How do I update my base resume after a new job?
Two options. (1) Edit Resume tab — manually add a new entry to the experience array, save. (2) Click New Resume in the header and re-upload your latest resume — note this overwrites everything.
Can I export multiple versions of my tailored resume?
Yes — change the filename in Settings before each download. Each download creates new files; previous ones aren't overwritten on disk.
I closed my browser and lost everything
If you cleared cookies/site data, the engine wipes localStorage and starts fresh. Always download a Word copy of your resume after major edits so you can re-upload if needed.
